Resumable Edge Upload as Reliability Infrastructure: A PiTUSK Study

Author: David Rinaldi · Working Paper (2026) · Category: IIoT Data Transport, Resilience, Operational Reliability

Abstract

This paper conceptualizes PiTUSK as a resilience layer for industrial-edge data transport. Instead of restarting full uploads after unstable links, resumable sessions preserve progress, reduce wasted transfer budget, and lower operator interruption. The contribution is an operationally testable protocol for chunked upload, offset recovery, and state validation in constrained deployments.

Research Problem

Conventional upload workflows on unstable links frequently force full retransmission, creating avoidable delays and operator overhead. In industrial operations, repeated retransmissions can disrupt control data availability and reduce service reliability. PiTUSK explores whether resumable transport can reduce such losses without increasing implementation complexity.

Research Questions

  1. How much transfer recovery improvement can resumable sessions provide compared with monolithic upload behavior?
  2. Can resumable mechanics be coupled with operational observability for practical incident handling?
  3. What reliability indicators are most meaningful for comparing edge upload reliability across environments?

Methodology

  • Design: implementation and field-oriented case study.
  • Units of analysis: upload sessions, offsets, state recovery events, and finalize outcomes.
  • Intervention: transition from full replay workflow to resumable stateful workflow.
  • Environment: edge node deployments under intermittent network behavior.

Data and Metrics

Core metrics are defined for external replication:

  • Restart Avoidance Rate (RAR): percentage of interrupted sessions resumed without full retransmission.
  • Recovery Time: delay from interruption event to resumed upload progress.
  • Operator Touch Count: manual intervention events per 100 upload sessions.
  • Integrity Completion Rate: ratio of sessions finalized successfully against created sessions.

Findings

Observed operational outcomes include:

  • Significant reduction of redundant transfer work under intermittent links.
  • More predictable recovery workflows because session state remains queryable through standard endpoints.
  • Clearer operational visibility for on-call teams through structured endpoint status responses.

Discussion

This study supports a practical research direction: upload reliability on edge should be treated as a first-class research object, not only as transport implementation detail. State durability, resumable offsets, and observability should be tested together with business impact metrics.

Threats to Validity

Threats include non-randomized traffic pattern selection, non-standardized file-size distributions, and limited hardware diversity in early trials. External replication on different industrial networks is essential before broad generalization.

Research Contribution

PiTUSK evidence supports reusable concepts for:

  • Reliable data transfer at edge nodes where links are intermittent.
  • Designing API contracts that keep resumability and operability coherent.
  • Defining research metrics for practical continuity and operator impact.
